Gabriela Thomas (Gabi) is studying Film & Media Arts (FMA) and Visual Anthropology at Temple University in Philadelphia, PA. She originally felt drawn to film making after attending the largest and most diverse high school in Philadelphia-- Northeast High. It was there that she made friends with people from all over the world and learned the realities of different cultures. Through the stories told by her peers, she felt inspired to learn more and educate others about the worlds beyond the United Sates.

From a very young age, Gabi felt captivated by the sight and sounds of cinema. It is no question that film has the same effect on other people, and therefore, has the power to move societies forward. Which explains why Gabi felt most compelled to use this medium to get her messages across.

However, while pursuing a General Degree in FMA, Gabi has discovered new mediums of media-- including Animation, Sound Design, and Web Art & Design. Rather than limiting her creations to only the movie screen, Gabi looks forward to creating a range of media content in the hopes to reach more audiences and make a larger impact on society.

Through the creation of her own videos and projects, Gabi has learned a number of skills, including cinematography, editing, lighting, and producing.

Over the course of the past two semesters, Gabi has gotten more involved in her community. She is currently working as a Co-Executive Producer on Megchen Niu's Graduate Thesis Film, Besieged. Meanwhile, she is also the intern Videographer at Al-Bustan Seeds of Culture, a non-profit program geared towards sharing Middle Eastern Art and Culture with the City of Philadelphia.
